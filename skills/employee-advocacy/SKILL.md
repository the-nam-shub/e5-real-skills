# Employee Advocacy

## Overview
This skill covers how B2B marketing teams can build, activate, and scale employee advocacy programs — from enabling individual contributors to post on LinkedIn, to running coordinated company-wide content campaigns. All practices are sourced exclusively from Exit Five podcast guests across 14 episodes. No general best practices have been added beyond what guests explicitly recommended.

---

## Foundational Philosophy: Why Employee Advocacy Works

Before executing tactics, establish the strategic rationale with your user:

- In B2B, differentiation increasingly comes from the **people behind the brand**, not just product features. Competitors can copy features; they cannot easily copy your team's authentic voices and relationships. Encourage employees and founders to build personal brands and share their work publicly — this creates a moat that compounds over time. (Source: Amanda Goetz, Episodes #244 and #158)

- Building brand around **multiple voices** reduces dangerous dependency on any single founder or executive. Empower dozens or hundreds of employees to create content authentically so the brand survives personnel changes. (Source: Dan Cmejla, Episode #134)

- When multiple employees post, the combined effect exceeds the sum of its parts. Think of it like a band: the company is the band, different people play different roles, and the lead singer will naturally get more visibility than the drummer — and that's fine. Don't try to grow all profiles equally. The aggregate impact ("one plus one plus one equals four") is what matters. (Source: Chris Walker, Episode #139)

## Building the Right Team for Advocacy

When advising on team composition and hiring:

- **Prioritize hiring marketing practitioners who have already built their own audience and reputation** — through LinkedIn, speaking, writing, or previous company visibility. These people bring credibility, an existing network, and the ability to represent your brand authentically. This matters especially for small teams where each hire wears multiple hats. (Source: Jess Cook, Episode #266)

- When an employee builds a strong personal brand, they become more valuable and have more options. **Retain them the way you'd retain a top sales rep or CTO**: competitive compensation, equity, and long-term incentives. Don't avoid building their personal brand out of fear they'll leave — ensure they're incentivized to stay. The company benefits from their brand-building on the way up, so invest accordingly. (Source: Chris Walker, Episode #139)

## Enabling Employees to Post: Practical Activation

### Starting with the Founder, Then Expanding

- Begin with the founder posting consistently on LinkedIn, then expand to other team members: CMO, head of brand, sales leaders, customer success managers. This creates multiple touchpoints in the feed, makes the company appear "everywhere," and brings diverse perspectives to your audience. Treat multi-person posting as a 201-level tactic that significantly amplifies reach compared to founder-only posting. (Source: Tommy Clark, Episode #171)

- Build a LinkedIn organic content strategy where the **founder, marketing leader, and other team members** (e.g., SDRs) post regularly about lessons learned from their daily work. Each person should post in their authentic voice and lane — founder posts about sales strategy, marketer posts about data and facts, SDR posts with personality and humor. (Source: Taylor Udell, Episode #190)

### Enabling Non-Marketing Employees

- **Model good content practices for engineers, customer success, and product teams.** Show them what a good LinkedIn post looks like, then help them adapt that framework to their specific area of expertise. This turns your entire company into a marketing engine and builds authentic credibility across multiple voices. (Source: Jess Cook, Episode #266)

- Empower your marketing team to share what they're learning — including failures and successes — on LinkedIn, X, Reddit, and other platforms. This builds authentic brand presence, demonstrates thought leadership, and creates multiple touchpoints for prospects researching your category. (Source: Dave Steer, Episode #324)

- Extend social media and thought leadership strategy beyond founders to **all employees**. Provide them with tools and content to make sharing easy. (Source: Ido Mart, Episode #229)

### Enabling the Sales Team Specifically

- **Train your sales team to build personal brands and share content** about your industry, products, and customer problems. Sales reps have direct conversations with prospects and understand pain points deeply — this makes them credible content creators. When they share on LinkedIn or X, they multiply your brand's reach across their networks. Sales reps with strong personal brands close deals faster and build stronger customer relationships. (Source: Ross Simmonds, Episode #209)

- Use a specific workflow to convert sales knowledge into social content: ask sales reps to identify the most frequently asked questions they hear on prospect calls, have them **record voice notes** answering those FAQs in their own words, paste the transcription into ChatGPT with the prompt: *"Write me a quick LinkedIn post describing what I said in a way that will work on LinkedIn,"* then edit the output to remove generic AI phrasing (e.g., "In the ever-evolving world of...") and adjust to match the salesperson's tone. (Source: Ross Simmonds, Episode #209)

## Content Infrastructure: The Company Page + Employee Amplification Model

- Use the **company LinkedIn page as a central news feed** for all content and product updates. Focus on zero-click content — a standalone insight or takeaway that readers get without clicking a link. Employees then amplify by reposting from their personal pages with their own perspective or connection to the content. This two-layer approach (company page + employee amplification) increases reach and engagement while maintaining a consistent company voice. (Source: Amanda Natividad, Episode #205)

## Campaign Tactics: Coordinated Advocacy Moments

### Company-Wide Video Takeovers

- For major product launches or announcements, **ask employees across all departments to record short videos (under 1 minute)** explaining why the launch matters. Provide a script and talking points but allow flexibility. Share all videos on the same day to create a coordinated wave of content across employee networks. This amplifies reach exponentially without paid media and requires genuine company buy-in and excitement about the product or initiative. (Source: Dave Gerhardt, Episode #134)

### Incentivized Sharing Contests

- Run **paid incentivized contests** where employees compete to get the most social shares on campaign content (particularly high-quality "Banger" content). Provide employees with starter copy, links, and images, but encourage them to personalize the message. Offer **cash prizes** to winners. This approach extends reach beyond company channels, leverages employee networks, and motivates participation through financial incentive. It works best when the content is genuinely good and employees are proud to share it. Follow up with sales 1–2 weeks later to contact engaged prospects. (Source: Erin May, Episode #337)

## Amplifying Employee Content with Paid Media

- **Boost personal posts as thought leader ads**, especially video content featuring founders, CEOs, or other visible leaders. Thought leader ads (boosting posts from individuals rather than company pages) generate significantly higher engagement rates and dwell time than company page ads. You can boost posts from employees, external influencers, or customers. The personal nature of the content drives higher engagement, lower CPMs, and more time spent reading. Thought leader ads don't require the person to be famous — even internal leaders can be effective. (Source: Anthony Blatner, Episodes #243 and #152)

- **Match the persona of the person being boosted to your target audience personas.** If targeting sales leaders, boost content from your VP of Sales. This drives higher engagement than company-page ads and builds personal credibility. (Source: Anthony Blatner, Episode #152)

- Combine organic multi-voice LinkedIn content with **thought leadership ads targeting your ICP** to amplify the best-performing organic posts. (Source: Taylor Udell, Episode #190)

## Measuring Employee Advocacy Impact

- Track **LinkedIn impressions on the company page before and after implementing employee advocacy**. Calculate the number of employee distribution points (employees posting). Compare company page impressions to total impressions across all employee posts. This shows the multiplier effect of employee advocacy on brand reach. (Source: Dan Cmejla, Episode #134)

## What NOT To Do

- **Do not limit social media and thought leadership strategy to founders only.** Restricting posting to one person caps your reach and creates dangerous single-point-of-failure dependency on that individual. (Source: Ido Mart, Episode #229; Dan Cmejla, Episode #134; Tommy Clark, Episode #171)

- **Do not try to grow all employee profiles equally.** Uneven growth across team members is normal and expected — accept it rather than fighting it. The lead singer will always get more visibility than the drummer. (Source: Chris Walker, Episode #139)

- **Do not avoid building an employee's personal brand out of fear they'll leave.** The answer to that risk is proper retention incentives, not suppressing their visibility. (Source: Chris Walker, Episode #139)

- **Do not run incentivized sharing contests with mediocre content.** The tactic works best when the content is genuinely good and employees are proud to share it. Forcing employees to share content they don't believe in undermines authenticity. (Source: Erin May, Episode #337)

- **Do not use generic AI-generated phrasing in employee social posts.** When using ChatGPT to help sales reps turn voice notes into LinkedIn posts, always edit out phrases like "In the ever-evolving world of..." and adjust to match the individual's authentic tone. (Source: Ross Simmonds, Episode #209)

- **Do not post only links on the company LinkedIn page.** Focus on zero-click content — standalone insights that deliver value without requiring a click — to maximize organic reach before asking employees to amplify. (Source: Amanda Natividad, Episode #205)
