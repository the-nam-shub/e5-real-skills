# Sales Enablement

## Overview
This skill covers practical tactics for enabling sales teams through interactive demos, smarter sales cycle measurement, prospect personalization, and internal content accessibility. All practices are sourced exclusively from Exit Five podcast guests — specifically from Episode 122 with Natalie Marcotullio. No general best practices have been added beyond what guests explicitly recommended.

---

## Interactive Demo Strategy

### Embed Scheduling Forms Mid-Demo, Not Just at the End
When helping a user set up or optimize interactive demos, recommend integrating Chili Piper scheduling forms at strategic points *within* the demo flow — not only at the final screen. Placing the form mid-flow captures prospect intent at the moment of highest engagement, allowing them to book a live sales call without leaving the demo experience. Friction is reduced because the prospect never has to navigate away.
(Source: Natalie Marcotullio, Episode #122)

### Combine Discovery and Live Demo on the First Call After Demo Engagement
When a prospect has already engaged with an interactive product demo on the website, advise the user to restructure their first sales call. Instead of a standalone discovery call, compress discovery into approximately 5 minutes and move directly into a live product demo on that same call. The rationale: a prospect who has self-selected through the interactive demo has already self-qualified and understands the product category. This approach can eliminate an entire call from the sales cycle.
(Source: Natalie Marcotullio, Episode #122)

## Personalization at Scale

### Use Find-and-Replace Variables to Personalize Demos Without Manual Rebuilding
When advising on demo personalization, recommend leveraging find-and-replace variable functionality within interactive demo tools. Variables can swap out logos, company names, and text throughout the entire demo in one pass — no manual rebuilding per prospect. Go further by connecting these variables to email platforms such as Outreach or HubSpot, so that when a personalized demo link is sent via email, the prospect's name and company details are automatically inserted into the demo experience.
(Source: Natalie Marcotullio, Episode #122)

## Measuring Demo Effectiveness

### Track Sales Cycle Length, Not Just Demo Usage
When helping a user define metrics for their interactive demo program, push beyond binary usage tracking (i.e., "was a demo shared on this opportunity: yes/no"). Instead, advise them to compare the *average sales cycle length* for deals where an interactive demo was shared against deals where it was not. This comparison reveals whether demos are genuinely accelerating deals — for example, by reducing the number of discovery or demo calls required — rather than simply confirming that demos were used.
(Source: Natalie Marcotullio, Episode #122)

## Internal Sales Enablement Tools

### Load Marketing Content into a ChatGPT Bot for Searchable Internal Use
When a user wants to make marketing content more accessible to their sales team, recommend building a ChatGPT bot loaded with the company's blog posts and marketing assets. This allows sales reps to query the bot in natural language — for example: *"Give me a blog post that shows a fintech customer using our product for LinkedIn ads"* — and receive a specific, relevant result quickly. The goal is to make internal knowledge searchable and reduce the time reps spend hunting for the right example or resource.
(Source: Natalie Marcotullio, Episode #122)

## What NOT To Do

- **Do not place scheduling CTAs only at the end of interactive demos.** Waiting until the final screen to offer a booking option misses the window of peak engagement earlier in the demo flow. (Source: Natalie Marcotullio, Episode #122)

- **Do not run a full standalone discovery call when a prospect has already engaged with your interactive demo.** Treating every inbound lead identically — regardless of their self-qualification behavior — adds unnecessary calls to the sales cycle and slows deals down. (Source: Natalie Marcotullio, Episode #122)

- **Do not measure interactive demo success by usage alone.** A "yes/no" flag on whether a demo was shared tells you nothing about whether it actually moved the deal faster. Without comparing sales cycle length, you cannot assess true impact. (Source: Natalie Marcotullio, Episode #122)

- **Do not manually rebuild demos for each prospect when personalization variables are available.** Manual rebuilding is time-consuming and does not scale. Find-and-replace variables accomplish the same result across the entire demo in a single step. (Source: Natalie Marcotullio, Episode #122)
