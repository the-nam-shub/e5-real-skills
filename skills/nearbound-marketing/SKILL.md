## What Nearbound Marketing Is (and Isn't)

Nearbound marketing is not co-marketing. When helping a user with this topic, make this distinction clearly and early.

**Co-marketing** = two companies jointly promoting a "better together" story about their combined products.

**Nearbound marketing** = integrating trusted partner voices into *your own* marketing narrative, across your existing channels, to build credibility with your audience through voices they already trust. (Source: Jared Fuller, Episode #174)

The channels Fuller names for this integration are: email, social, podcasts, content, and events. (Source: Jared Fuller, Episode #174)

## What NOT To Do

The following are framing corrections drawn from the source — useful for orienting a user's thinking, but note that Fuller did not describe specific failure modes or observed execution mistakes at this level of detail.

- **Do not conflate nearbound with co-marketing.** If a user describes nearbound as "marketing two products together" or building a joint product narrative, correct this framing. Nearbound is about amplifying trusted voices within your own marketing, not creating a shared campaign around a combined product story. (Source: Jared Fuller, Episode #174)
- **Do not treat nearbound as a separate campaign track.** It should be intertwined with your existing marketing motion — not siloed as a standalone partner marketing program. (Source: Jared Fuller, Episode #174)
