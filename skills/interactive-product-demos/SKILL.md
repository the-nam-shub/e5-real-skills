# Interactive Product Demos

## Overview
This skill covers best practices for creating, deploying, personalizing, and measuring interactive product demos in B2B marketing contexts. It addresses demo structure, placement strategy, lead capture, sales cycle integration, and channel distribution. All practices are sourced exclusively from Exit Five podcast guests — specifically Natalie Marcotullio on Episode 122 (March 4, 2024).

---

## Demo Structure and Content

**Keep demos short and focused on aha moments.**
Structure interactive product demos to be 8–15 steps, taking approximately 30 seconds to complete. This length is sufficient to surface major value moments without revealing your entire product or giving competitors a complete blueprint. Treat the demo as an appetizer, not the full meal. (Source: Natalie Marcotullio, Episode #122)

**Edit demo data to tell a better product story.**
Use the demo editor to clean up or modify the product data shown in the demo. If your product UI contains messy data, negative numbers, or incomplete states, edit the demo to show only the polished, ideal end-state — for example, replacing negative metrics with positive ones or surfacing only the most compelling dashboards. You do not need a perfect demo environment to produce a compelling demo. (Source: Natalie Marcotullio, Episode #122)

## Placement and Distribution

**Place ungated demos prominently above the fold.**
Position the interactive demo as a secondary CTA (e.g., "Take a Tour") alongside your primary CTA (e.g., "Get a Demo"). Place it above the fold and in the navigation bar. Data shows 84% of top-performing demos use this placement strategy, and ungated demos drive 40–60% visitor engagement compared to 10–15% for product videos. (Source: Natalie Marcotullio, Episode #122)

**Embed demos on third-party review sites.**
Place interactive product demos on G2 and other review site product pages. Prospects visiting these pages are in active evaluation mode — comparing you directly against competitors. An interactive demo lets you demonstrate differentiation in the moment rather than relying on written descriptions alone. (Source: Natalie Marcotullio, Episode #122)

**Drive LinkedIn ad traffic to demo landing pages.**
When running LinkedIn ads, direct traffic to a landing page featuring the interactive demo rather than asking users to book a sales call directly. LinkedIn users are in a passive browsing mindset and respond better to low-friction engagement. The demo serves as a self-serve entry point before a prospect commits to a sales conversation. (Source: Natalie Marcotullio, Episode #122)

**Use interactive demos in email campaigns.**
Embed or link to interactive demos in email campaigns, particularly for product-led growth onboarding sequences. Use demos to re-engage users who haven't logged in after day one by educating them about the product outside the product itself. Demos can also anchor nurture campaigns around specific features or use cases. (Source: Natalie Marcotullio, Episode #122)

**Deploy demos as part of feature launch campaigns.**
Use interactive demos wherever you would normally use a video or screenshot in a feature announcement. This gives prospects and customers a hands-on way to understand new functionality rather than reading about it. (Source: Natalie Marcotullio, Episode #122)

**Use demos for async customer onboarding and training.**
Deploy interactive demos as an asynchronous training resource for existing customers. This reduces the need for one-on-one training sessions and allows customers to learn product features at their own pace. (Source: Natalie Marcotullio, Episode #122)

## Personalization

**Build a demo center with multiple variants by persona and use case.**
Rather than a single generic overview demo, create separate interactive demos for different personas and use cases. Present these in a demo center where prospects can self-select the demo most relevant to their role or problem (e.g., via a modal asking about their use case). This creates a personalized experience without requiring a gate upfront. (Source: Natalie Marcotullio, Episode #122)

**Use find-and-replace variables to personalize demos at scale.**
Leverage find-and-replace functionality to swap logos, company names, and text throughout a demo using variables — without manually rebuilding each demo for each prospect. Integrate with email platforms like Outreach or HubSpot so that when a personalized demo link is sent via email, the prospect's name and company details are automatically inserted. (Source: Natalie Marcotullio, Episode #122)

## Lead Capture and Gating

**Embed multiple CTAs throughout the demo, not just at the end.**
Place 2 or more calls-to-action (e.g., "Schedule a Demo," "Contact Us") at strategic points throughout the demo rather than only at the final step. Top-performing demos achieve 30–32% click-through rates on embedded CTAs. Track these conversions using UTM parameters. (Source: Natalie Marcotullio, Episode #122)

**Embed scheduling forms mid-demo to capture leads at peak intent.**
Integrate Chili Piper scheduling forms directly into the demo at strategic mid-flow points — not only at the end. This allows prospects to book a live demo with a sales rep without leaving the interactive demo experience, capturing intent at the moment of highest engagement. (Source: Natalie Marcotullio, Episode #122)

**If gating, delay the gate until step five.**
If you must gate the demo to capture lead information, do not require it upfront. Place the gate at step five of the demo instead. This approach yields 15% higher engagement rates because prospects experience product value before being asked for their information, reducing friction while still capturing leads. (Source: Natalie Marcotullio, Episode #122)

**Track account-level intent from ungated demos using IP and cookie tracking.**
Even when demos are fully ungated, use IP and cookie tracking to identify which accounts engaged with the demo and which specific demos or features they viewed. This first-party intent data can inform personalized outreach and account-based marketing efforts without requiring a form fill. (Source: Natalie Marcotullio, Episode #122)

## Sales Cycle Integration

**Combine discovery and live demo on the first call after demo engagement.**
When a prospect has already engaged with an interactive demo on your website, skip the traditional discovery-only first call. Instead, run a brief 5-minute discovery followed immediately by a live product demo on the same call. The prospect has already self-qualified by viewing the demo and understands what they're evaluating. This approach can eliminate an entire call from the sales cycle. (Source: Natalie Marcotullio, Episode #122)

**Measure demo impact by tracking sales cycle length, not just usage.**
Do not limit measurement to whether a demo was used in an opportunity (yes/no). Instead, compare the average sales cycle length for deals where an interactive demo was shared versus deals where it was not. This reveals whether demos are actually accelerating deal velocity by reducing the need for additional discovery or demo calls. (Source: Natalie Marcotullio, Episode #122)

## What NOT To Do

- **Do not place the demo only at the end of the page or only at the end of the demo flow.** Burying CTAs and the demo itself reduces engagement. Top-performing demos are above the fold and in the navigation. (Source: Natalie Marcotullio, Episode #122)
- **Do not gate the demo upfront.** Requiring a form fill before a prospect sees any product value significantly reduces engagement. If gating is necessary, delay it until step five. (Source: Natalie Marcotullio, Episode #122)
- **Do not build a single generic demo and call it done.** A one-size-fits-all demo fails to address different personas and use cases. Build a demo center with multiple variants. (Source: Natalie Marcotullio, Episode #122)
- **Do not show a messy or unpolished product state in demos.** Use the demo editor to clean up data and present the ideal end-state. A poor visual presentation undermines the product story. (Source: Natalie Marcotullio, Episode #122)
- **Do not run a full discovery-only first call with prospects who have already engaged with your interactive demo.** This wastes the self-qualification signal the prospect has already provided and adds unnecessary friction to the sales cycle. (Source: Natalie Marcotullio, Episode #122)
- **Do not measure demo success only by whether it was used in a deal.** Binary usage tracking misses the more meaningful question of whether demos are shortening sales cycles. (Source: Natalie Marcotullio, Episode #122)
- **Do not ask LinkedIn users to book a demo directly from an ad.** LinkedIn users are in a passive browsing mindset. Drive them to a demo landing page first. (Source: Natalie Marcotullio, Episode #122)
