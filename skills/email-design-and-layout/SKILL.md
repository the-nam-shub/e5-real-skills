# Email Design and Layout

## Overview
This skill covers structural and visual best practices for B2B marketing emails, including text formatting, link management, CTA placement, file size constraints, and emerging considerations like AI inbox summaries. All practices are sourced exclusively from Exit Five podcast guests across 6 episodes. No general marketing knowledge has been added — gaps in coverage are intentional.

---

## Text Formatting and Readability

**Keep every text block to 3–4 lines maximum.**
This is one of the most consistently reinforced practices across multiple guests. Blocks of 5 or more lines trigger subconscious avoidance — readers immediately tune out, similar to how they react to long text messages. This applies even when the overall email is long; visual formatting matters more than total length. Break content into shorter blocks using bullet points, different colors, or visual dividers to maintain engagement. On mobile, two-line desktop text can become five lines, so err on the shorter side.
(Source: Jay Schwedelson, Episode #329; Jay Schwedelson, Episode #241; Jay Schwedelson, Episode #167)

**Use white space, bold text, and clear section headers to structure content.**
Don't focus only on words — structure and format matter as much as the copy itself. Break content into clearly labeled sections. Use white space and short paragraphs. Format should make the email visually appealing and easy to scan, because people process visual presentation before they read.
(Source: Dave Gerhardt, Episode #314)

**When hyperlinking, make the entire sentence or phrase the hyperlink and bold it — not just the word "here."**
Linking only a single word like "here" is a missed opportunity for visual emphasis and click surface. Bold the full linked phrase so it stands out as a clear, scannable action.
(Source: Dave Gerhardt, Episode #314)

## Inbox Presence and Open Rate Optimization

**Use short subject lines (3 words or fewer) and suppress preview text to create white space in the inbox.**
When you don't populate the preview text field, most email systems default to showing the first line of body text. Instead, use your email platform's documentation or a tool like ChatGPT to insert hidden code or a hidden image that prevents any preview text from displaying. Combined with a short subject line, this creates stark white space around your email in the inbox — making it stand out since approximately 98% of emails include preview text. This tactic is reported to significantly increase open rates.
(Source: Jay Schwedelson, Episode #329)

## Link Management and Deliverability

**Minimize the total number of links in any single email.**
Spam filters treat multiple links as a phishing signal, which harms deliverability. If you need to reference multiple resources, tease the content in the email body and ask recipients to reply if they want the links. High engagement from an opted-in list can partially offset the negative impact of multiple links, but minimizing link count is the safer default.
(Source: Alex Fine, Episode #256)

**When multiple links are unavoidable, consolidate them into a single master link to a landing page.**
Instead of linking to 8–12 individual items (e.g., individual job listings), list the items with brief descriptions in the email body and include one link to a page where recipients can explore all options. This reduces link count, protects deliverability, and preserves user experience.
(Source: Alex Fine, Episode #256)

## CTA Placement and Design

**Make your call-to-action visually distinct, clearly clickable, and placed above the fold.**
If your CTA looks like footer text or blends into the background, recipients won't click it. Buttons should look like buttons — visually prominent and easy to identify at a glance. Test that your CTA is immediately recognizable without scrolling.
(Source: Sheri Otto, Episode #247)

## Technical Constraints

**Keep email file size under 102 kilobytes to avoid Gmail clipping.**
Gmail clips emails that exceed 102 KB. When an email is clipped, critical elements — including unsubscribe links — may not render, creating compliance risk and a poor user experience. Optimize and compress your email code during the build process and test file size before sending.
(Source: Jaina Mistry, Episode #241)

**Include sufficient live (non-image) text so AI inbox summaries accurately represent your email.**
AI-generated email summaries are becoming standard in inbox clients. Emails that rely heavily on images or have minimal live text will produce poor or irrelevant AI summaries, which can hurt open rates. Treat live text as a technical requirement, not just a design preference.
(Source: Jaina Mistry, Episode #241)

## Visual Design and Animation

**Use animated GIFs strategically, but always test the first frame.**
Animated GIFs can elevate email design when used subtly. However, some email clients only render the first frame of an animated GIF. Always test to ensure the first frame is meaningful and not confusing or visually broken on its own. Never assume animation will display as intended across all clients.
(Source: Pierce Uijainwalla, Episode #167)

**Use creative design transitions (curved lines, flowing elements) to break up monotonous straight-line layouts.**
Subtle HTML design transitions between sections — such as curved dividers or flowing visual elements — add visual interest and help your email stand out from typical corporate layouts. Use these to elevate design without overwhelming the content.
(Source: Pierce Uijainwalla, Episode #167)

## What NOT To Do

- **Do not use text blocks longer than 4 lines.** Large blocks cause readers to skip content entirely, regardless of how compelling the message is. (Source: Jay Schwedelson, Episodes #329, #241, #167)
- **Do not populate the preview text field if you want to stand out in the inbox.** Leaving it populated makes your email look like the other 98% of emails. Use hidden code to suppress it intentionally. (Source: Jay Schwedelson, Episode #329)
- **Do not include 8–12 individual links in a single email.** This signals phishing behavior to spam filters and damages deliverability. (Source: Alex Fine, Episode #256)
- **Do not bury your CTA below the fold or style it so it blends into surrounding content.** If it doesn't look like a button, it won't get clicked. (Source: Sheri Otto, Episode #247)
- **Do not let your email file size exceed 102 KB.** Gmail will clip the email, potentially hiding your unsubscribe link and creating compliance exposure. (Source: Jaina Mistry, Episode #241)
- **Do not build image-only emails or emails with minimal live text.** AI inbox summaries will misrepresent your content, hurting open rates. (Source: Jaina Mistry, Episode #241)
- **Do not use animated GIFs without testing the first frame.** If the first frame is meaningless or broken, that's what many recipients will see. (Source: Pierce Uijainwalla, Episode #167)
- **Do not link only the word "here."** Make the full phrase the hyperlink and bold it for visibility and click surface. (Source: Dave Gerhardt, Episode #314)
