# Marketing Metrics and Measurement

## Overview
This skill covers how B2B marketers should select, structure, and report on marketing metrics — from foundational measurement infrastructure to executive reporting to channel-specific diagnostics. All practices are sourced exclusively from Exit Five podcast guests across 4 episodes. Do not supplement with general knowledge; if a topic is not covered here, acknowledge the gap rather than invent guidance.

---

## Foundational Measurement Infrastructure

**Establish clean data and measurement before optimizing anything else.**

When a user is trying to reduce CAC, optimize paid media, or improve channel performance, first ask whether they have clean tracking, attribution, and lead routing in place. If they don't, direct them to fix measurement infrastructure before attempting optimization. Most companies lack visibility into what's actually working, which forces gut-based decisions. Proper tracking is the biggest lever for identifying wasted spend — not creative testing or channel switching.

> "Implement proper tracking, attribution, and lead routing before attempting channel optimization or creative testing." (Source: Eli Rubel, Episode #120)

**Map the revenue supply chain before setting marketing metrics.**

Help users create a visual map of the complete revenue generation process — from initial site visit through customer payment — and explicitly identify:
- Which stages **marketing wholly owns** (e.g., site visits, demo requests)
- Which stages **marketing influences but does not control** (e.g., deal qualification, competitive positioning)

Use this map to set goals: optimize aggressively on owned stages, and support influenced stages with enablement assets (battle cards, competitive positioning, etc.). This framework prevents marketing from optimizing for metrics that don't connect to revenue, and surfaces where cross-functional collaboration is required.

> (Source: Brian Kotlyar, Episode #118)

## Executive and Board Reporting

**Report revenue or revenue-influenced metrics to executives — not intermediate funnel metrics.**

When helping a user prepare CEO or board-level marketing reports, do not recommend reporting demo volume, MQL count, or other intermediate metrics as primary KPIs. Instead, guide them to report on:
- New revenue added
- All-up revenue
- The specific metrics in their revenue supply chain that marketing directly owns (e.g., site visits, demo requests)

Explain the risk clearly: vanity metrics can make marketing look successful even when the business is underperforming, and they create misalignment with what leadership actually cares about.

> "Vanity metrics create misalignment because they don't reflect what the company actually cares about (revenue), and they can make marketing look successful even when the business is failing." (Source: Brian Kotlyar, Episode #118)

## Diagnosing Performance Gaps

**Diagnose email performance by isolating which layer is broken before optimizing.**

When a user reports that nurture emails have high open rates but low conversion, walk them through this diagnostic logic:

- **High opens + low conversion** → The subject line is working; the problem is the email body, CTA, or offer
- **Low opens** → The subject line is the problem
- **High opens + low conversion in a PLG context** → Content or CTA problem
- **High opens + low conversion in an enterprise context** → May indicate timing mismatch (prospect isn't ready to buy), not a messaging problem

Do not recommend optimizing subject lines when the open rate is already strong. Identify which layer is broken first.

> (Source: Eli Rubel, Episode #120)

**Use the Demand Efficiency Benchmark to surface optimization opportunities across the buyer journey.**

When a user wants to improve marketing program performance without a major overhaul, recommend a structured evaluation across categories including messaging alignment, experimentation, customer focus, and funnel coverage. This surfaces specific, actionable gaps — such as missing lead routing, absent mid-funnel nurture sequences, or lack of use-case-specific messaging — that can be prioritized and addressed incrementally.

> (Source: Eli Rubel, Episode #120)

## Channel-Specific Measurement

### Interactive Demos

**Measure interactive demo impact by tracking sales cycle length, not just usage.**

When a user asks how to measure the effectiveness of interactive demos, do not let them stop at a binary "was a demo used in this opportunity?" metric. Guide them to compare:
- Average sales cycle length for deals **where an interactive demo was shared**
- Average sales cycle length for deals **where it was not**

This reveals whether demos are actually accelerating deals by reducing the need for additional discovery or demo calls — which is the business outcome that matters.

> (Source: Natalie Marcotullio, Episode #122)

**Track account-level intent from ungated demos using IP and cookie tracking.**

When a user has ungated interactive demos and worries about losing lead capture data, explain that they can still generate first-party intent signals without a form. IP and cookie tracking can identify which accounts engaged with specific demos, revealing interest in particular features or use cases. This data can inform personalized outreach and ABM prioritization.

> (Source: Natalie Marcotullio, Episode #122)

### Social Media and Content

**In early-stage social growth, measure output metrics — not engagement metrics.**

When a user with a small or early-stage social presence asks what metrics to track, redirect them away from engagement rates, impressions, and virality. Instead, have them track proactive output metrics they control:
- Number of posts published
- Blog posts written
- Influencer videos created

Engagement metrics only become meaningful at scale, when there is enough data to benchmark against. Focusing on output reduces pressure, enables experimentation, and builds the consistency needed to eventually generate meaningful engagement data.

> (Source: Ross Simmonds, Episode #121)

## What NOT To Do

- **Do not report MQL count, demo volume, or other intermediate funnel metrics as primary KPIs to executives or the board.** These metrics can mask business underperformance and create misalignment. (Source: Brian Kotlyar, Episode #118)
- **Do not attempt to optimize paid media or reduce CAC before establishing clean tracking, attribution, and lead routing.** Optimization without measurement infrastructure produces gut-based decisions, not data-driven ones. (Source: Eli Rubel, Episode #120)
- **Do not optimize email subject lines when open rates are already high.** Diagnose which layer of the funnel is broken before prescribing a fix. (Source: Eli Rubel, Episode #120)
- **Do not measure interactive demo effectiveness with a binary used/not-used flag.** This misses the actual business impact — sales cycle acceleration. (Source: Natalie Marcotullio, Episode #122)
- **Do not obsess over engagement or virality metrics when your social audience is small.** Without scale, these metrics are not meaningful benchmarks. (Source: Ross Simmonds, Episode #121)
- **Do not assume ungated content means no measurement.** IP and cookie tracking can recover intent signals even without form fills. (Source: Natalie Marcotullio, Episode #122)
