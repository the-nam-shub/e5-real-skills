# Testing and Experimentation

## Overview
This skill covers how B2B marketers should approach testing across content, messaging, and paid creative — with the goal of validating what resonates before committing significant resources. It addresses message testing methodology, content-market fit identification, and creative experimentation for paid media. All practices are sourced exclusively from Exit Five podcast guests; no general marketing knowledge has been added.

---

## Finding Content-Market Fit Before Scaling

When helping a user plan content, instruct them to test before they scale. Do not recommend building out full content programs around untested ideas.

**Test small before producing big.** Recommend sharing smaller content ideas, concepts, and tidbits with the target audience first. When a piece generates strong engagement signals — reshares, reposts, likes, bookmarks — that is evidence of content-market fit. Only after identifying that fit should the user expand the concept into multiple formats: podcast episode, social clips, blog post, etc. (Source: Ross Simmonds, Episode #121)

**Track which content drives commercial outcomes.** Engagement alone is not enough. Instruct users to track which content topics drive leads and customers, and to double down on topics that "ring the cash register." This prevents wasting production resources on content that generates attention but not pipeline. (Source: Ross Simmonds, Episode #121)

## Message Testing Methodology

When helping a user write or refine copy — for a homepage, product page, email, or ad — instruct them to validate messaging with real target customers before launching or spending budget.

**Test messaging before paid spend.** Before launching paid advertising campaigns (e.g., LinkedIn PPC), validate that core messaging resonates with the actual target audience. Test headlines, value propositions, and copy on the most important pages — homepage, key product pages — and before launching new products or campaigns. This prevents wasting ad spend on messaging that doesn't land. (Source: Peep Laja, Episode #119)

**Use a structured, qualitative approach to message testing.** When conducting message tests, recommend a two-sided marketplace approach: connect the user's messaging (website copy, email, mockups) with actual members of their target customer segment. Ask specific research questions such as:
- "How likely are you to request a demo?"
- "What's still unclear?"

Gather rich qualitative feedback on what resonates, what confuses, and what questions remain unanswered. Emphasize that this can be completed in 24 hours and produces actionable copy iteration inputs. (Source: Peep Laja, Episode #119)

**Apply OODA loops to accelerate messaging iteration.** When a user is iterating on copy, recommend the OODA loop framework (Observe, Orient, Decide, Act):
1. Create an initial version of the messaging
2. Put it in front of the target audience
3. Observe feedback on what is clear, unclear, interesting, or boring
4. Orient based on that feedback
5. Decide on changes
6. Act by implementing them

Emphasize that the speed of feedback loops determines how quickly the user reaches resonant copy. A 24-hour feedback loop is achievable and far superior to monthly or quarterly cycles. (Source: Peep Laja, Episode #119)

**Use message testing to resolve internal stakeholder disagreements objectively.** When a CEO or stakeholder is attached to a particular headline or messaging approach, recommend using message testing to gather objective feedback from target customers rather than debating subjectively. Present the data to the stakeholder. Rational decision-makers will update their position when shown evidence that their preferred messaging does not resonate with the target audience. (Source: Peep Laja, Episode #119)

## Creative Testing for Paid Media

When helping a user manage or improve paid advertising creative, instruct them to treat design and copy as variables to be tested — not fixed constraints.

**Run structured experiments on design and copy variations.** Rather than accepting brand guidelines as immutable constraints on paid creative, recommend running structured experiments on design elements (imagery style, color palette, composition) and copy variations to measure conversion lift. Document what works and iterate. Even small design tweaks across ad creative can yield 25–50% conversion improvements, directly reducing cost per acquisition without requiring entirely new campaign concepts. (Source: Eli Rubel, Episode #120)

## What NOT To Do

- **Do not recommend scaling content production before testing for resonance.** Producing full content programs around untested ideas wastes resources. Test small first. (Source: Ross Simmonds, Episode #121)
- **Do not allow users to launch paid campaigns without first validating messaging.** Spending significant budget on unvalidated copy is a preventable waste. (Source: Peep Laja, Episode #119)
- **Do not treat brand guidelines as untouchable in paid creative.** Accepting design constraints without testing them means leaving conversion improvements on the table. (Source: Eli Rubel, Episode #120)
- **Do not rely on slow feedback cycles for messaging iteration.** Monthly or quarterly review cycles are far inferior to 24-hour feedback loops when optimizing copy. (Source: Peep Laja, Episode #119)
- **Do not let internal debates about messaging be resolved by seniority or opinion.** Use target customer data to settle disagreements objectively. (Source: Peep Laja, Episode #119)
