# Enterprise GTM Strategy

## Overview
This skill covers go-to-market strategy for companies moving upmarket into enterprise, including how to convert organic product adoption into top-down sales conversations and how to set realistic expectations for the transition timeline. All practices are sourced exclusively from Exit Five podcast guests and reflect their direct experience leading enterprise marketing motions. The single contributing episode featured two guests — Emma Robinson and Kristine Segrist — who contributed distinct practices from different functional vantage points.

---

## Converting Organic Adoption into Enterprise Pipeline

When a product already has widespread grassroots usage inside large organizations, use that adoption as the foundation for top-down enterprise conversations rather than treating enterprise as a cold outreach motion.

- In C-suite outreach emails or decks, lead with the organic adoption statistic before any product pitch — frame it as employee-validated proof rather than a vendor claim. Then position the enterprise offering as a way to accelerate, govern, and structure that organic usage, not as a net-new tool requiring adoption from scratch. This lowers perceived risk for decision makers. (Source: Emma Robinson, Episode #277)

---

## Setting Realistic Timelines for the Enterprise Transition

The shift from consumer or self-serve to enterprise is a structural change in how the market perceives your brand — and structural changes take time.

- When presenting to leadership, use a recognized company's multi-year credibility build as an anchor before sharing your own roadmap. Emma Robinson cited Google's transition to Google Cloud as an example of this kind of long-term arc. Deploy this framing in leadership or board settings specifically, before quarterly reviews where short-term variance might otherwise trigger premature strategy pivots. (Source: Emma Robinson, Episode #277)

## What NOT To Do

- Do not allow quarterly variance in enterprise pipeline growth to trigger a strategy pivot. Some quarters will show significant uplift, others slower growth — this is normal during a multi-year transition. Treating the process as a sprint, or abandoning the approach when early quarters underperform, undermines the long-term credibility build that enterprise GTM requires. (Source: Emma Robinson, Episode #277)
