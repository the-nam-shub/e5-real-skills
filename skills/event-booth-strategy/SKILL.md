# Event Booth Strategy

## Overview
This skill covers how B2B marketers can design booth experiences that drive engagement, create memorable brand moments, and generate qualified interest at trade shows and sponsored events. All practices are sourced exclusively from Exit Five podcast guests across 2 episodes. No general marketing knowledge has been added.

---

## Move Beyond Swag: Build Experiences Instead

The clearest theme across guest advice is that branded swag (hoodies, hats, stickers) is a weak use of booth budget. Replace it with activations that attendees will remember, talk about, and seek out.

- **Create interactive, product-centric experiences** that let attendees directly engage with what you sell. At Abundance360, HeyGen partnered with Proto Holograms to display interactive avatars in a hologram machine and built an avatar creation station where executives could generate their own digital twins. This drove engagement and created qualified interest without requiring attendees to hand over contact information first. (Source: Holly Xiao, Episode #270)

- **Design experiential activations that generate word-of-mouth.** Examples include tarot card readings, live pancake art featuring attendee likenesses, and cold brew coffee service — anything interactive and personal that attendees will describe to colleagues on the show floor. (Source: Stephanie Christensen, Episode #227)

---

## Booth Design: Stand Out Visually

- **Invest in custom booth design rather than accepting generic templates.** Thoughtful layout, strong branding, and distinctive visual elements draw attendees in and create a professional impression that differentiates you from competitors on a crowded trade show floor. (Source: Kristina DeBrito, Episode #227)

---

## Pre-Event Outreach: Drive Traffic Before the Doors Open

- **Send a physical item to target attendees before the event** that ties directly to your booth activation. If your booth features a Plinko game, mail attendees a Plinko chip in advance with a note telling them where to find you. This creates anticipation, gives recipients a reason to seek out your booth specifically, and makes the experience feel personal before it begins. (Source: Stephanie Christensen, Episode #227)

---

## What NOT To Do

- **Do not rely on branded swag as your primary booth draw.** Hoodies, hats, and stickers create no meaningful brand recall and do not differentiate you from the dozens of other booths giving away the same items. (Source: Stephanie Christensen, Episode #227)

- **Do not accept a generic booth template.** Blending into the trade show floor is a missed opportunity. If your booth looks like everyone else's, attendees have no visual reason to stop. (Source: Kristina DeBrito, Episode #227)

- **Do not gate engagement behind contact information collection as the first step.** Letting attendees experience the product before asking for anything creates stronger qualified interest than requiring a badge scan to participate. (Source: Holly Xiao, Episode #270)
