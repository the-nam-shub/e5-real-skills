# Episode #341: B2B Ad Campaigns That Actually Worked (From Five Demand Gen Marketers)

This episode assembled five demand gen practitioners to walk through specific campaigns they ran, with enough operational detail to be genuinely useful. It broke meaningful new ground on multi-channel audience architecture, direct mail sequencing, and conference plays without booths.

## New practices extracted

- **Influencer-led product launch with paid amplification.** Goldcast's Cindy Dubon recruited three to four genuine product users as influencers, gave them a loose brief, and amplified their organic posts with paid ads targeted at sales-prioritized accounts and retargeting segments. The campaign returned $700K in pipeline on $8K in ad spend. The key constraint: influencers must actually use the product. Testing confirmed that non-users underperform consistently.

- **Surround sound across CTV, YouTube, and LinkedIn from a single ICP audience.** Vector's Kelly Arndt built one contact-level audience of 120K to 180K and deployed it simultaneously across LinkedIn Connected TV, YouTube, and LinkedIn brand solution and thought leader ads, with different creative on each channel. She validated creative on organic before spending paid budget, targeting a 30%+ YouTube view-through rate as the quality threshold. Early results showed 34% net new traffic, 22% more sales demo requests, and 41% pipeline growth month over month.

- **Direct mail with QR-triggered retargeting sequence.** Jeremy Chung sent $1.50 postcards with unique tracked QR codes to 1,200 VC GPs and managing directors. Scanning the QR code triggered a Loom video audit and enrolled recipients in a 21-day sequence of email, LinkedIn retargeting ads, and thought leader ads. Only QR scanners received the premium $35 follow-up booklet. The campaign produced 22 meetings at roughly $500 cost per meeting, with deals averaging $9K per month.

- **Conference presence without a booth.** AirVet's Tess Pfeifle ran LinkedIn ads featuring branded product creative (a dog pen outperformed a generic "Let's talk" CTA) to conference attendees and nearby target accounts, uploaded via Vector and LinkedIn. A landing page tracked visits even though it generated zero form conversions, which Vector surfaced. Post-event, the team emailed only those who had visited the page. Results: 65% of conference attendees visited the site (versus a typical 35% booth traffic benchmark), 11 on-site meetings, 5 post-event meetings, 3 open deals, and 2,000%+ ROI on $11K total spend.

- **Claude-assisted competitive audit via ad libraries.** Chung used Claude to systematically analyze Meta's ad transparency library and Google's ad transparency library for portfolio companies, extracting messaging patterns, tech stack signals, and conversion problems. This research fed directly into outbound personalization and Loom audit content.

- **CTV retargeting audience built within LinkedIn.** Arndt noted that LinkedIn's Connected TV campaigns (run under the brand awareness objective) allow you to build a retargeting audience from viewers directly inside LinkedIn, which can then be used in subsequent thought leader or brand solution campaigns. This closes the loop between CTV exposure and lower-funnel LinkedIn touchpoints.

- **Intent-signal-based ICP audience construction.** Dubon used a tool called Play to identify companies exhibiting behavioral signals aligned with Goldcast's ICP: video on their website, webinar activity, event participation, and job changes. This produced a more precise target list than firmographic filters alone.

- **Offer-to-ad alignment as a conversion prerequisite.** Dubon's campaign drove to a free trial signup with no sales conversation required. The framing here is structural: the offer must be the natural next step from the ad, not a friction-adding detour through a sales rep. When the offer matched the ad, conversion followed.
