# Episode #347: Chris Cunningham on How ClickUp Built a B2B Social Machine

This episode covered ClickUp's social media operation in granular operational detail, from weekly production cadence to platform distribution logic. It broke meaningful new ground on execution specifics while reinforcing several existing positions on brand investment, AI content, and measurement patience.

## New practices extracted

- **Separate accounts by content type.** ClickUp runs distinct accounts for product content, comedy, man-on-the-street interviews, and memes. The comedy account outperforms the main brand account because audiences self-select for entertainment. This prevents the main account from being diluted and allows each property to grow independently.

- **Weekly writers room and shoot day cycle.** Monday is ideas and voting (no ego, majority rules), Tuesday is script refinement and casting, Wednesday is quality check, Thursday is a full shoot day producing 12 to 15 videos, Friday is performance analysis. This flywheel keeps the content bank 4 to 6 weeks ahead of publishing.

- **Instagram Trial Reels for pre-publish A/B testing.** Create three variations of each video with different hooks or text overlays (roughly four minutes of additional editing per variant), post all three to Trial Reels before publishing to followers, and release only the winner. Cunningham reports this lifted a video from 60K impressions to 10 to 20 million. The variation you think will win is frequently not the one that does.

- **ICP interviews as a standing content research practice.** Schedule 15-minute recorded calls with 2 to 3 ICPs per week, sourced from both the customer base and cold LinkedIn outreach. Spend the first seven minutes asking what they find funny, what podcasts they watch, and what absurd things have happened to them at work. Offer edited clips in return. This is how Cunningham generates authentic content ideas rather than guessing.

- **Facebook Reels as an underutilized B2B distribution channel.** ClickUp generated roughly 100 million impressions from Facebook Reels in a single month, approximately half their total monthly impressions. The platform skews toward the 38 to 60 age range, which maps well to B2B decision-makers. The practice is simply to post the same short-form video content there alongside Instagram and TikTok.

- **Recruit emerging creators by finding small accounts with viral hits.** Search for creators with 3K to 8K followers who have produced one to three videos with over one million views. These creators have demonstrated talent but are earning nothing and are waiting for a brand to reach out. Approach via DM with a specific, genuine compliment about their work, offer performance-based pay increases tied to growth goals, and build loyalty by being their first real opportunity.

- **Create for two platforms, distribute everywhere.** Rather than producing unique content per platform, select two primary creation targets (Cunningham uses TikTok/Instagram Reels for short-form video and LinkedIn for thought leadership) and distribute that same content across all other channels. This reduces production complexity without sacrificing reach.

- **Rule of three as a content gate.** Before posting anything, ask whether it makes the audience feel something, teaches them something, or makes them laugh. If it satisfies none of those criteria, do not post it. This is the mechanism Cunningham uses to reject internal requests for event announcements and product-only posts.

## Skills updated

**social-media-strategy-and-operations** received the largest single-episode update in recent history, with 12 new practices added. The most operationally specific additions are the Trial Reels testing workflow, the multi-account architecture, and the create-for-two/distribute-everywhere framework.

**content-operations** gained five new practices covering the weekly production cycle, post frequency testing, batch shooting, iPhone-first production, and scheduling tooling (Vista Social). The weekly writers room cadence is the most detailed production workflow this file has received from any guest.

**video-content-marketing** was updated with four practices, reinforcing the existing lean toward iPhone and authenticity while adding the Trial Reels testing method and the batch production approach as new operational specifics.

**creator-led-content-programs** (currently at 8 total practices) received two new entries on identifying and recruiting emerging creators. This is a thin file and these additions represent a meaningful proportional increase.

**brand-measurement-and-roi** and **channel-and-campaign-measurement** both received the social-via-sales-feedback proxy metric and the 6-month patience recommendation, adding practitioner evidence to what had been a more abstract discussion of brand attribution.

**content-ideation** and **customer-research** were both updated with the ICP interview practice, which is more operationally specific than most existing entries in either file.

The gap has widened.

The single-channel-first position still holds a 3 vs. 1 advantage, though Cunningham's is the most recent data point and comes from a larger, more mature operation.
